Texas Slave Narratives
Born in Slavery: Slave Narratives from the Federal Writers’ Project, 1936-1938
Julian Blanks’ Narrative
I was born in San Antonio, in 1862. My mother's name was Rachael Miller, I don't know if she was born in Tennessee or Mississippi, I heard her talk of both places. I don't know nothing about my father, because he run off when I was about three months old. He was three-quarter Cherokee Indian. They were lots of Indians then…

You see, my grandmother was free born, but they stole her and sold her to Miss Donaldson. She was half French. She looked jes’ like a French woman. She wasn't a slave, but she and her brother were stolen and sold.

James Boyd’s Narrative
I’s born in dat Phantom Valley, in de Indian Territory, what am now call Oklahoma. Us live in a Indian hut. My pappy Blue Bull Bird and mammy Nancy Will. She come to de Indian Territory with Santa Anna, from Mississippi, and pappy raise in de Territory. I don' 'member much bout my folks, 'cause I stole from dem when I a real li'l feller.

William Davis’ Narrative
My Pappy was a Indian, I knows dat. He comes from Congo, over in Africa and I heard him say a big storm drove de ship somewhere on de Ca'lina coast, I 'member he mighty ‘spectful to Massa and Missy, but he proud too, and walk straighter than anybody I ever seen. He had scars on de right side he head and cheek what he say am tribe marks, but what dey means I don't know.

James Cape Narrative
I was born in yonder southeast Texas…my mammy and pappy was born in Africa, dats what they told me.
