Runaway Slave Notices
What It's About: Notices offering rewards for the capture of runaway slaves.

Slave owners placed these notices in their local paper in hopes of catching runaways. In early Texas, some men made a living capturing slaves and turning them in for the posted reward.
Excerpt from Frederick Law Olmsted’s Journal
What It's About: Runaway Slaves in Mexico

Olmsted was commissioned to travel across the south and report about the conditions of slavery. His accounts of the Texas frontier include a trip into Mexico where he met runaway slaves.
Texas Slave Narratives
What It's About: Leaving the Plantation

In the 1930s the Federal Writers’ Project sent people to interview former slaves. These narratives are a rich source of information about life in slavery.
