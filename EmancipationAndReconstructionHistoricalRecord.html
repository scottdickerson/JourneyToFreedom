Texas Slave Narratives
What It's About: Emancipation

In the 1930s the Federal Writers’ Project sent people to interview former slaves. These narratives are a rich source of information about life in slavery.
Freedmen’s Bureau Records - Texas
What It's About: Violence Against Freedmen and Union Men

The Union soldiers running the Freedmen’s Bureau faced threats and violence as did the freedmen they sought to protect. These records detail a few illustrative cases.
Advice to Freedmen Published in a Newspaper
What It's About: Getting A Home

The Free Man’s Press was a newspaper published by in Austin, Texas in the years after Emancipation. It was reported to be the first newspaper for African Americans in Texas. Jacob Fontaine, an influential African American minister, served as editor.
